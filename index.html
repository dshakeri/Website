SFO RFP

This proposal won The Allen Group, LLC a $92 million contract with SFO Airport for the demolition of the old Air Traffic Control Tower and office spaces above Terminal 2.

I wrote many sections myself and interviewed teammates whose input was key (though their skill set is engineering more so than writing and “translated” their input into prose. I edited the final product, integrating content from multiple teammates, to ensure the entire document reads as a cohesive Whole with a single voice.

One of the biggest challenges in writing proposals for a government agency is answering all questions within the limited page count allowed. For this proposal, I also worked with my team to create charts and graphics we felt would effectively convey our point and save us space and word count.

SFO ATCT

The construction of the new Air Traffic Control Tower project included funding from the Federal Aviation Admnistration (FAA) as well as the City of San Francisco and was therefore subject to public audit. Before publishing, the City Services Auditor provided her report to SFO Airport, allowing the Airport’s formal response to be included in the final publication.

Operating as the Owner’s Representative, The Allen Group, LLC assisted in crafting SFO’s formal response. The construction manager for The Allen Group wrote a rough first draft, which I significantly edited and submitted to my team for feedback before subsequent revisions. The final, published draft is largely a product of my own writing.

RFI Software Process

The process for writing RFIs includes multiple software programs. We use BlueBeam for viewing and modifying PDFs and Viewpoint Team for document transmittal. The process also spans several project players, including the general contract, architect, consultants, and all subcontractors. I created this flow chart documenting the RFI process to ensure all stakeholders understand the process clearly. It’s also intended as a training tool as new engineers and interns are brought on to the project and require onboarding.

RFI 530

The purpose of a project engineer in construction is to coordinate the execution of the architect’s design by multiple subcontractors. This includes creating project documents (called Requests for Information, or RFIs) that call on the architect to modify design details that are not constructible.

I wrote the attached RFI for a $170 million apartment project in South Lake Union. It requests that the architect modify many of the design details around the kitchen casework (cabinets) to allow for field tolerance and to provide proper ventilation for refrigerators. Once answered, the RFI and architect’s response are incorporated into the project documents and supersede previous design.
